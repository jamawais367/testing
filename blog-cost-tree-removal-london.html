Cost of Tree Removal in London

A clear guide to what affects pricing for tree removal in London and how to budget for safe, compliant work.

Key cost factors

Tree removal costs in London vary widely due to factors such as tree size, species, condition, access, location, and whether additional services like stump grinding or traffic management are required. Urban properties frequently present logistical challenges: narrow access, proximity to buildings, roots under paved surfaces, and local council regulations. Large mature trees require more labour, time and potentially specialist equipment such as cranes or telehandlers, which increases costs. Emergency call-outs outside normal hours also attract higher fees. When requesting quotes, ensure the arborist includes all line items: assessment, labour, equipment, waste disposal and any additional permits or traffic management.

Average price ranges

While prices change over time, a useful rule-of-thumb in London is:

  • Small tree (up to 6m): £150–£400
  • Medium tree (6–12m): £400–£1,200
  • Large tree (12m+): £1,200–£4,000+
  • Stump grinding: £80–£400 depending on size and access

Conservation areas, TPOs or restricted access can increase costs due to permit applications or specialist equipment requirements.

How to get a fair quote

Get multiple written quotes and ensure the arborist visits the site. Look for transparent, itemised pricing and confirm public liability insurance and qualifications. Ask about waste disposal and whether the quote includes chip removal or stump grinding. For complex jobs, a detailed method statement and timeline are important. If the property is in a conservation area or the tree has a TPO, confirm who will handle council applications or emergency exemptions.

Value and long-term considerations

Choosing the cheapest option isn’t always best. Professional removal reduces risk to people and property and ensures legal compliance. Consider long-term value: stump grinding avoids future regrowth, and proper disposal reduces biosecurity risks. A reputable company will provide a written report and clear aftercare recommendations.
